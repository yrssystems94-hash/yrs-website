What's Actually Involved

What a Complete Re-Roof Includes

"Re-roofing" means replacing the entire roofing system, not just the visible shingles. A proper job includes:

  • Full tear-off of old shingles and underlayment down to the decking
  • Decking inspection and repair — replacing any rotted or damaged plywood found underneath
  • New underlayment and flashing at every valley, chimney, and roof-to-wall junction
  • Proper attic ventilation to prevent the heat buildup that shortens shingle life and contributes to ice dams
  • New shingles, installed to manufacturer spec rather than laid directly over old material

Skipping any of these steps is how a "new roof" ends up with the same problems as the old one within a few years.

When Re-Roofing Makes More Sense Than a Repair

Full replacement is the right call when damage is spread across multiple areas of the roof, the shingles are already past their expected 20–30 year lifespan, or repeated repairs are just patching a roof that's fundamentally worn out. We diagnose the actual condition of your roof before recommending replacement over a repair — not the other way around.

Quick Answers

Complete re-roofing questions

How long does a complete roof replacement take?

Most residential re-roofs are completed in one to a few days, depending on the size of the roof, the number of layers being removed, and weather conditions.

Do I need to be home during the re-roof?

No. Most homeowners aren't home for the full job. We handle the work, protect the property, and clean up thoroughly, including disposal of the old roofing material.

What's the difference between re-roofing and a repair?

A repair fixes a specific, contained problem. Complete re-roofing replaces the entire roofing system down to the decking, addressing widespread wear or a roof past its expected lifespan.

Will old roofing material be disposed of properly?

Yes. Full tear-off and disposal of old shingles and underlayment is included as part of a complete re-roofing job.
