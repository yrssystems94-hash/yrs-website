What Causes Shingle Damage

Shingle damage rarely comes out of nowhere. The most common causes we see in Kitchener-Waterloo are:

  • Wind uplift — loosening or tearing shingles at the edges and ridge
  • Hail and storm impact — cracking or knocking granules loose, exposing the shingle underneath
  • Age-related wear — granule loss and curling as shingles approach the end of their 20–30 year lifespan
  • Improper original installation — nailing patterns or sealant strips that never bonded correctly

Signs You Need Shingle Repair

Missing shingles, visible cracking or curling, granules collecting in the gutters, or a section of roof that looks visibly different from the rest are all signs worth a proper inspection before they turn into a leak.

Repair vs. Replacement for Shingles

Localized shingle damage — a handful of missing or cracked shingles, one storm-affected slope — almost always just needs a targeted repair. Full replacement only makes sense when damage is spread across the whole roof or the shingles are already past their expected lifespan. We inspect before we recommend, so you're never sold a bigger job than you actually need.

Our Shingle Repair Process

We match the color, style, and profile of your existing shingles as closely as possible, replace the damaged sections, and reseal and inspect surrounding areas so the repair holds through the next storm — not just until the next one.

Shingle repair questions

Can you match my existing shingles?

In most cases, yes. We match the color, style, and profile of your existing shingles as closely as possible so the repair blends in.

How quickly can a shingle repair be done?

Most localized shingle repairs are completed in a single visit. Emergency shingle damage from storms is prioritized for same-day response.

What causes shingles to crack, curl, or go missing?

Wind uplift, hail impact, age-related granule loss, and improper original installation are the most common causes in Kitchener-Waterloo.

Does storm damage usually need a full roof replacement?

Not usually. Storm damage is often limited to a specific area and can be repaired directly. We inspect the full roof before recommending anything beyond a targeted repair.
